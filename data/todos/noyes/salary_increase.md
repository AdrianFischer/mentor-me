# Salary Increase
<!-- id: salary_increase_migrated -->
#noyes

Context
	•	You’re a high performer / “rising star” who has sometimes threatened your boss’s position (politically), but your boss still thinks highly of you and supports you.
	•	You want the next salary jump and expect to justify it not only to your boss but also to your boss’s boss.
	•	Your boss also wants a pending salary increase, so your cases may be linked.
	•	New constraint: you already earn ~40% more than peers, while still outperforming them.
	•	Likely near-term role change: you may become Team Lead soon.
	•	Lifestyle constraint: you may need more home office soon due to building a house (land bought), and you’re unsure Team Lead will be best for work–life balance.

⸻

Key principle from “Never Outshine the Master” (applied)
	•	Don’t make your boss feel competed-with; make him look like the sponsor/architect of your success.
	•	Create a story where your growth = proof of your boss’s leadership, not a threat.
	•	In skip-level conversations, keep your boss central and credited.

⸻

Recommended negotiation framing (given you’re already +40% vs peers)

Avoid: “I’m better than everyone / I deserve more.”
Use: “My scope/level has changed; comp should align to role + leverage + risk reduction.”

Because you’re already above peers, “equity/market adjustment” is weaker. Best path:
	•	Package the request as a promotion/level alignment (e.g., Team Lead or Staff/Principal path), not just a raise.

⸻

Core argument pillars for approval (“survive scrutiny”)

Approvers (skip-level/HR/finance) will check:
	•	pay band/level alignment, internal equity, budgets, precedent, evidence.

So build your case on:
	1.	Expanded scope
	•	responsibilities you own end-to-end, decisions without supervision, leadership/process ownership.
	2.	Business impact
	•	measurable outcomes: revenue, cost savings, cycle time, quality, risk reduction, throughput.
	3.	Retention/market reality (tactful)
	•	market movement / ongoing recruiter interest (only if true), framed calmly (no threats).

⸻

Political strategy: make it a shared win with your boss
	•	Ask your boss to lead the case upward; you provide a clean “packet” he can forward.
	•	Tie your case to his: “This promotion/comp alignment strengthens the org and supports retention—also evidence of effective leadership.”

⸻

“Choice architecture” to make approval easy

Offer options so leadership can say yes within constraints:
	•	Option A: Team Lead promotion + base aligned to the new band.
	•	Option B: Smaller base now + written 6-month review tied to clear metrics.
	•	Option C: If base is constrained: one-time bonus/retention bonus + promotion now + base catch-up next cycle.

⸻

Skip-level (boss’s boss) approach
	•	Position it as growth + future value, not ego/performance.
	•	“My manager and I are aligned on my scope; I’m asking for formalization + alignment.”
	•	Bring a 90-day plan (2–3 outcomes, metrics, stakeholders, risks/mitigations).

Important: do not repeat politically risky comparisons (e.g., “my 4 hours > principal candidate”). Translate to safe language:
	•	“High-impact outcomes, low oversight, de-risking delivery, multiplying team effectiveness.”

⸻

Work–life balance / Home office negotiation (bundle it)

Since you expect to need more home office:
	•	Negotiate remote/hybrid explicitly as part of the promotion package (not vague).
	•	Offer clear availability for leadership duties and outcome-based expectations.
	•	Protect WLB in Team Lead role with operating rules: async updates, fewer standing meetings, maker-time blocks, delegation, on-call boundaries.

⸻

Suggested talk tracks (summary)

To your boss
	•	“I’m ready for formal Team Lead scope; I want you to sponsor the promotion + comp alignment. I’ll send a one-pager you can forward. I also want to lock in a sustainable home-office model.”

To your boss’s boss
	•	“My manager and I are aligned that I’m moving into Team Lead scope. I’m asking to formalize role + align compensation. Here’s the 90-day plan and operating model.”

⸻

Main risks to avoid
	•	Any hint of “promote/raise me or else.”
	•	Comparing yourself to your boss or peers.
	•	Overemphasizing effort; underemphasizing scope/impact/level alignment.
	•	Going around your boss (unless he blocks you; currently he seems supportive).

⸻

Deliverable you planned next (for a future agent)
	•	A one-page promotion/comp packet containing:
	•	Current scope vs Team Lead scope
	•	3–5 quantified impacts
	•	90-day outcomes + metrics
	•	Requested package options (A/B/C)
	•	Proposed home-office arrangement + leadership availability plan
